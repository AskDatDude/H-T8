    General instructions and Tips

    Creating Engaging Project Articles


    • Make It Personal:
      • Frame the project as a personal journey or diary entry.
      • Focus on your own experiences, challenges, and learning moments.
      • Highlight what excites or motivates you about the project.
    • Be Clear and Concise:
      • Use numbered steps for clarity.
      • Avoid unnecessary jargon unless explained.
    • Include Visuals:
      • Incorporate diagrams, screenshots, or videos to enhance understanding.
    • Provide Realistic Goals:
      • Set achievable objectives and milestones.
      • Break down complex tasks into manageable steps.

    Project Development Stages

    • Idea Generation:
      • Brainstorm potential topics that align with your personal interests, current trends or take inspirations from science fiction. (The projects don't have to be production ready products but rather, demonstrate your imagination, skills and interests.)
      • Identify skills or technologies you want to explore and evolve in.
    • Planning:
      • Set clear objectives, key features, and development timeline for the project.
      • Break down the project into smaller tasks that can be assembeled together when finished.
    • Development:
      • Implement the project features and functionalities based on the plan. Don't add features that were not in the plan. (Rather write them up, and use these ideas to develop the project further in the future.)
      • Test and validate the project at each stage to ensure it works as intended.
    • Presentation:
      • Share outcomes in club meetings or forums.
      • Encourage discussions and don't be afraid to ask for feedback.
